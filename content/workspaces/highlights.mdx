# Highlights

The Highlights workspace is where you capture meaningful moments -- achievements, milestones, experiences, and memories worth remembering. Each highlight carries an importance level so you can distinguish everyday wins from life-changing events.

<Callout type="info">
Think of Highlights as your personal hall of fame. When you look back months or years from now, these are the entries that tell the story of what mattered most.
</Callout>

---

## Purpose

Highlights are designed for moments that stand out from your daily routine:

- **Achievements** -- Completed a project, hit a fitness goal, shipped a feature
- **Milestones** -- Birthdays, anniversaries, promotions, graduations
- **Experiences** -- A concert that moved you, a conversation that changed your thinking
- **Gratitude moments** -- Times you felt especially thankful or lucky
- **Small victories** -- Overcame a fear, handled a difficult situation well

---

## Metadata

Highlights have one unique metadata field beyond the shared fields (title, content, tags, people, date):

### Importance Level

Rate each highlight by how significant it is:

| Level | When to Use | Examples |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **High** | Life-changing or rare events | Got promoted, bought a house, had a child |
| **Medium** | Notable moments worth remembering | Great dinner with friends, finished a book, personal best at the gym |
| **Low** | Small wins and everyday positives | Good coffee, nice weather walk, compliment from a stranger |

Set the importance level from the metadata dropdown on the Write page. It defaults to no selection, so you can leave it unset if you prefer.

<Callout>
Don't overthink importance levels. They are a rough filter, not a precise ranking. The goal is to make it easier to find your biggest moments when browsing later.
</Callout>

## Creating a Highlight

<Steps>
### Open the Write Page

Navigate to `/write` or press `Ctrl/Cmd + W`.

### Select "Highlight" Type

Use the entry type dropdown at the top of the editor and choose **Highlight**.

You can also go directly to `/write?type=highlight`.

### Set Importance (Optional)

Choose High, Medium, or Low from the importance dropdown.

### Write Your Entry

Describe the moment. Use `#tags` for context and `@mentions` for people involved.

```markdown
Finally finished the marathon today! 26.2 miles in 4:12:33.

Months of training with @Coach-Mike paid off. The last 6 miles
were brutal but the crowd energy at mile 20 carried me through.

#running #marathon #achievement #fitness
```

### Auto-Save

Your highlight saves automatically. Check the cloud indicator in the header.
</Steps>

## The Highlights Workspace Page

Navigate to **Highlights** in the sidebar or go to `/highlights` to view all your highlight entries.

### What You See

- **Entry cards** sorted by date, newest first
- **Importance indicator** displayed on each card (High, Medium, Low)
- **Content preview** showing the first few lines of each entry
- **Tags and people** visible at a glance
- **Star indicator** on starred entries

### Filtering

You can filter highlights by importance level to focus on your biggest moments or browse your everyday wins.

## Use Cases

  <Tabs.Tab>
    **Tracking Achievements**

    Record accomplishments as they happen so you never forget them:

    ```markdown
    Shipped v2.0 of the product today. Six months of work
    with @Alice and @Bob. Client response has been incredible.

    #work #launch #team-effort
    ```

    Set importance to **High** for career-defining wins and **Medium** for solid accomplishments.
  </Tabs.Tab>
  <Tabs.Tab>
    **Recording Milestones**

    Mark the dates that divide your life into before and after:

    ```markdown
    One year anniversary at the company today. Hard to believe
    how much has changed since I started. @Boss surprised me
    with a thoughtful card from the whole team.

    #work #anniversary #milestone
    ```

    These are almost always **High** importance.
  </Tabs.Tab>
  <Tabs.Tab>
    **Capturing Experiences**

    Write about moments that moved, surprised, or changed you:

    ```markdown
    Saw the northern lights for the first time tonight.
    Standing in the cold with @Partner, watching green waves
    ripple across the sky. No photo could capture it.

    #travel #nature #bucket-list
    ```

    Use any importance level based on how significant the experience felt.
  </Tabs.Tab>
  <Tabs.Tab>
    **Gratitude Highlights**

    Separate from daily gratitude journaling, these are moments of deep thankfulness:

    ```markdown

    sick. Didn't even stay long, just wanted to make sure
    I was okay. Sometimes the small things hit hardest.

    #family #gratitude #love
    ```

    Great for **Medium** or **High** importance.
  </Tabs.Tab>

## Tips for Effective Highlights

### 1. Capture It Now

Write highlights the same day they happen. Details fade quickly, and the emotional context is freshest right after the moment.

### 2. Include Sensory Details

What did you see, hear, or feel? Specific details make highlights vivid when you revisit them months later.

### 3. Tag People Involved

Use `@mentions` generously. When you look back, knowing who shared the moment with you adds meaning.

### 4. Use Importance Honestly

Not everything is **High** importance. Giving most highlights a **Medium** or **Low** rating makes the truly significant ones stand out.

### 5. Star the Best

Combine starring with importance for a two-tier system: importance rates the event, starring marks the entries you want to revisit most.

### 6. Review Monthly

Browse your highlights at the end of each month. You will often find you had a better month than you remembered.
