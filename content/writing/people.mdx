# People

People are the human connections in your reflection journey. Track relationships, interactions, and conversations by mentioning people in your entries with `@mentions`.

<Callout type="info">
People in Reflecto are automatically extracted from your entries and tracked across all entry types. See who you interact with most, when, and in what context.
</Callout>

---

## What Are People?

People are contacts mentioned in your entries using the `@` symbol.

### How People Work

1. **Write with @mentions** — Type `@Alice`, `@Bob`, `@Sarah` in your entries
2. **Auto-extraction** — People are extracted when you save
3. **Suggestion dropdown** — Existing people appear as you type
4. **Entry linking** — @mentions create bidirectional links between entries and people
5. **Private & scoped** — Your people list is completely private to you

### @Mention Syntax

```markdown
Had coffee with @Alice this morning.
She introduced me to @Bob from the #design team.
Later, @Sarah and I discussed #project-alpha.
```

When you save this entry, three people are automatically extracted:
- @Alice
- @Bob
- @Sarah

## Creating People

### Inline in Editor

The easiest way to add people is while writing:

<Steps>
### Type `@`

The suggestion dropdown appears instantly

### Start Typing

See existing people that match what you type

### Select Existing or Create New

- **Arrow keys** — Navigate suggestions
- **Enter** — Select highlighted person
- **Keep typing** — Create a new person
- **Space/Tab** — Finalize mention

### Person Is Created on Save

New people are added to your people library when you save the entry
</Steps>

### People Suggestions

As you type, the dropdown shows:
```
┌─────────────────────────────┐
│ @Alice (34 mentions)        │
│ @Alicia (7 mentions)        │
│ @Alan (2 mentions)          │
│ ───────────────────────────  │
│ Create "@Alexandra"         │
└─────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Matching people** — Filtered by what you've typed
- **Mention counts** — How many entries mention each person
- **Create new option** — Always available at the bottom

<Callout>
**Pro tip:** Check suggestions before creating new people to avoid duplicates (e.g., @Alice vs @AliceSmith).
</Callout>

## People Naming Best Practices

### Use Consistent Names

Decide how you'll refer to each person:
- ✅ `@Alice` — First name only
- ✅ `@Alice-Smith` — First + last with hyphen
- ❌ Don't mix: `@Alice`, `@AliceS`, `@Alice Smith`, `@A.Smith`

### Capitalize Properly

Names should use standard capitalization:
- ✅ `@Alice`, `@Bob`, `@Sarah`
- ❌ `@alice`, `@ALICE`, `@aLiCe`

### Use Hyphens for Full Names

If you need full names, use hyphens:
- ✅ `@Alice-Johnson`
- ❌ `@Alice Johnson` (space breaks the mention)

### Consider Context

Add context for common names:
- ✅ `@Mike-Work`, `@Mike-Family`
- ✅ `@Sarah-Designer`, `@Sarah-Manager`

### Professional vs Personal

You might want different naming conventions:
- **Work:** `@Alice-Smith` (formal)
- **Personal:** `@Mom`, `@Dad`, `@Sister` (familiar)

<Callout type="warning">
@mention names cannot contain spaces or special characters (except hyphens). Stick to letters, numbers, and hyphens.
</Callout>

## Editing People

### Renaming a Person

<Steps>
### Navigate to People Page

Click **People** in the sidebar

### Click the Person

Select the person you want to rename

### Click Edit Button

Found in the person detail view header

### Enter New Name

Type the new person name

### Confirm

Changes apply to all entries mentioning this person
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
Renaming a person updates them across **all entries**. This is useful for fixing typos or consolidating duplicate people.
</Callout>

### Merging People

To merge duplicate people:

1. Rename one person to match another
2. System detects duplicate
3. Confirms merge
4. All entries now mention the single person

**Example:**
- You have @Alice (30 mentions) and @Alice-Work (15 mentions)
- Rename @Alice-Work to @Alice
- Now @Alice has 45 mentions
- @Alice-Work no longer exists

## Deleting People

### When to Delete People

- **Typos** — Created by mistake (better to rename)
- **Irrelevant** — Person no longer in your life
- **Cleanup** — Consolidating duplicate entries

### How to Delete

<Steps>
### Navigate to People Page

Click **People** in the sidebar

### Click the Person

Open the person detail view

### Click Delete Button

Usually a trash icon

### Confirm Deletion

Warning appears: "This person will be removed from all entries"

### Person Is Deleted

Removed from your library and all entries
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
Deleting a person is **permanent** and removes them from all entries. Consider renaming instead if you might want the data later.
</Callout>

```markdown

Results:
- @Alice (45 mentions)
- @Alice-Work (12 mentions)
- @Alicia (7 mentions)
```

- **Instant filtering** — Results update as you type

When viewing a person's entries, filter by type:

```markdown
@Alice in entries:
```

### Date Range Filtering

View mentions within specific timeframes:
- **Last 7 days** — Recent interactions

## Best Practices

### 1. Be Consistent with Names

Decide on a naming convention early:
- First name only: @Alice
- First + last: @Alice-Johnson
- Role-based: @Boss, @Mentor
- Context: @Alice-Work, @Alice-Friend

### 2. Review People Monthly

Check the People page monthly:
- Merge duplicate entries
- Delete people no longer relevant
- Create groups for organization
- Identify who you're interacting with most

### 3. Use @Mentions Liberally

Don't just mention names — use @mentions for:
- Direct interactions
- References in conversation
- People you're thinking about
- Relationship reflections

### 4. Combine with Tags

People + Tags create rich context:
```markdown
Meeting with @Alice about #project-alpha and #design.
@Bob shared ideas on #marketing strategy.
```

### 5. Track Important Relationships

Create entries specifically about key relationships:
```markdown
Checking in on my relationship with @Partner.
Entry type: Journal
Category: Reflections
Tags: #relationships #love #growth
```

### 6. Use Groups Effectively

Once you have 15+ people, organize them:
- Work, Family, Friends
- Close vs. Acquaintances
- Active vs. Past relationships

### 7. Check Insights

Review people analytics to:
- See who you mention most
- Identify fading relationships
- Spot patterns in interactions
- Track relationship health

### 8. Mention Retroactively

Edit old entries to add @mentions you weren't using before. This builds richer relationship data.

## Relationship Insights Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Identifying Close Relationships

**Question:** Who am I closest to?

**Method:**
1. Go to People page
2. Sort by mention count (Most → Least)
3. Top 5-10 people are your closest connections

**Insights:**
- @Partner (89 mentions) — Primary relationship
- @Best-Friend (67 mentions) — Close friendship
- @Mom (54 mentions) — Family connection

### Scenario 2: Tracking Work Relationships

**Question:** How often do I interact with my team?

**Method:**
1. Create "Work Team" group
2. Add relevant people
3. Check mention frequency
4. Compare week-over-week

**Insights:**
- @Boss (12 mentions this month) — Regular 1-on-1s
- @Alice (18 mentions) — Close collaboration
- @Bob (4 mentions) — Less interaction, consider reaching out

### Scenario 3: Relationship Evolution

**Question:** How has my relationship with @Alice changed?

**Method:**
1. Navigate to @Alice's detail page
2. Filter by date (e.g., last 6 months)
3. Review entries chronologically
4. Note tag and mood correlations

**Insights:**
- Early: Mostly #work tags, neutral moods
- Recent: More #personal tags, positive moods
- Evolution: Work relationship → friendship

### Scenario 4: Finding Fading Relationships

**Question:** Who am I losing touch with?

**Method:**
1. Go to Insights → People tab
2. Check "Declining Mentions" chart
3. Identify people with decreasing mentions

**Action:**
- Reach out to reconnect
- Reflect on why contact decreased
- Decide if relationship is worth maintaining

## Privacy & Considerations

### Your Data Is Private

- People list is **completely private** to you
- No one else can see who you mention
- Names are not shared or visible to others
- Full control over your relationship data

### Ethical Considerations

**Be Mindful:**
- People mentioned are likely unaware they're in your journal
- Don't rely on Reflecto for relationship management alone
- Real communication with people is irreplaceable
- Use @mentions as reflection tools, not substitutes for connection

**Best Practices:**
- Write honestly but respectfully
- Reflect on relationships constructively
- Use insights to improve real-world connections
- Don't obsess over mention counts

<Callout type="warning">
Reflecto is a personal reflection tool. While tracking relationships can provide insights, it's not a replacement for genuine communication and connection with people in your life.
</Callout>

## Troubleshooting

### Person not appearing in suggestions?

- Type at least 2 characters after `@`
- Check that you've mentioned them before (in another entry)
- Verify entry was saved successfully

### Person not extracted from entry?

- Ensure name has `@` prefix
- Check there's no space after `@` (e.g., `@ Alice` won't work)
- Save the entry (people extract on save, not during typing)

### Can't delete a person?

- Ensure you have permission (shouldn't be an issue for your own data)
- Check browser console for errors
- Refresh the page and try again

### Mention count seems wrong?

- Counts update when entries are saved
- Refresh the People page
- Check that entries weren't deleted

### Groups not saving?

- Ensure you've selected multiple people
- Enter a group name
- Check internet connection
